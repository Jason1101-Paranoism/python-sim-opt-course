# Python Simulation & Optimization Pathway
## Research-grade projects for AP, IB, and international high school students

---

## Course Title and One-Line Pitch

**Python Simulation & Optimization Pathway**
*A structured, 4-level mentorship program that takes students from "I can write Python" to "I've completed a university-level research project."*

---

## Who This Is For

This program is designed for:

- **AP Research students** who want their research project to involve rigorous simulation or numerical modeling
- **IB students** working on Internal Assessments (IA) or Extended Essays (EE) with a quantitative, computational focus
- **A Level students** with coursework or independent study requirements in mathematics, physics, or computer science
- **International high school students** building a research portfolio for university applications (US, UK, or global)
- **Competitive students** preparing for STEM competitions, science fairs, or math modeling challenges
- **Parents and college counselors** looking for a structured, academically rigorous program that produces verifiable, student-authored work

**Prior knowledge required:** Basic Python (variables, loops, functions). No calculus or statistics required for Level 1 and 2.

---

## What You'll Build — Deliverables by Level

### Level 1: Introduction to Simulation
*10 weeks | Prerequisite: Basic Python*

- A working simulation program on a topic of your choice (probability games, queuing systems, population dynamics)
- A 1–2 page illustrated mini-report with results and analysis
- A short group simulation project presentation

### Level 2: AP/IB/A-Level Research Project
*10 weeks | Prerequisite: Level 1 or placement test*

- A complete research-style report aligned with AP Research, IB IA, or A Level coursework rubrics
- Full simulation program + all figures and data files
- An 8–10 minute research presentation with Q&A practice

### Level 3: Numerical Methods and Optimization
*10 weeks | Prerequisite: Level 2 or equivalent project experience*

- An advanced technical report using NumPy, SciPy, ODE solvers, or numerical optimization
- A structured portfolio (code with documentation + report + slides)
- A professional-level presentation suitable for competitions or portfolio review

### Level 4: Research / Capstone
*10 weeks | Prerequisite: Level 3 or strong project background*

- A 10–15 page research report (literature review, methodology, results, discussion, conclusion)
- A research presentation slide deck
- One full mock oral defense session with feedback

*Level 4 deliverables are directly suitable as writing samples for university applications, AP Research submissions, IB Extended Essays, or competitive research programs.*

## How It Works

### Format
Each level runs for **10 weeks** with two components per week:

| Session Type | Duration | Format |
|-------------|----------|--------|
| Group class (4–8 students) | 2 hours/week | Instruction, peer review, workshop |
| 1-on-1 mentorship | 1 hour/week | Individualized feedback on your project |

**Total per student: 30 hours per level** (20 hours group + 10 hours individual mentorship)

### Class Size
4–8 students per cohort. Classes do not run below 3 students.

### Language
- Level 1–2: Instruction in Chinese (Mandarin), with English terminology preserved and explained
- Level 3–4: Can switch to Chinese-English bilingual or full English upon request (recommended for international school students)

### Location
In-person at partner locations in Hsinchu, Taiwan. Remote sessions available for international students on request.

---

## Why This Is Different

### vs. Coding Bootcamps
Bootcamps teach syntax and tool usage. This program teaches you how to use Python to model and investigate a real problem you care about — and how to write about it rigorously. The output is a research document, not a portfolio of tutorial exercises.

### vs. Test Prep / APCS Classes
APCS and exam-focused classes optimize for test scores. This program is explicitly not a test-prep course. The goal is a research artifact you can show to universities, professors, and competition judges — something that demonstrates independent thinking.

### vs. Summer Project Camps
Two-to-four-week intensive camps often produce template-based outputs that all look similar. This program runs for 10 weeks with weekly 1-on-1 mentorship, giving each student time to develop and refine their own research question, model, and findings.

### vs. Ghost-Writing Services
This program does not produce work for students. Everything is done by the student, with the mentor acting as a research supervisor — asking questions, pointing to resources, reviewing drafts. The work is verifiable as the student's own, which matters for academic integrity in AP, IB, and university contexts.
